Type 5: The Investigator

5- The Investigator. It was named personality type Five The Investigator because, more than any other type, Fives desire to find out why things are the way they are. They seek to understand how the world operates, whether it is the cosmos, the microscopic world, the animal, vegetable, or mineral kingdoms—or the inner world of their imaginations. They are always searching, asking questions, and diving into things in depth. They do not accept received opinions and doctrines, feeling a strong need to find the truth of most assumptions for themselves.

Type 5 in Found in the Bible

Zacchaeus exhibits five qualities when he is shown to hoard and withhold with his material possessions.

He wants to learn and observe about Jesus, and does so from a distance, staying away from bodily contact with other people. This distance is disrupted when Jesus moves into his space, and as a result, Zacchaeus no longer lets his greed and hoarding define him.

He embodies the path to growth for fives when he begins to share all that he has stored up for a long time and opens up his home in hospitality.

Type 5 Common Sins

Greed- Fives have a strong desire to be competent and able. They value knowledge, understanding, and insight. When that is tested and feelings of incapability come up Fives may feel insecurity in not being able to function in the way they want to. Out of this insecurity can come a certain scramble to gain as much knowledge that they can to make them feel confident and prepared.

Encouragement: True knowledge and wisdom come from God. Your knowledge can be used to either defend you, or can be used to create something beautiful. What you do with your knowledge is up to you, but do not waste it on using it to feel competent, bur rather lay down those insecurities at the cross of Jesus Christ and let him use your knowledge to inspire people. “For the Lord gives wisdom; from his mouth come knowledge and understanding” (Proverbs 2:6)

Type 5 Freed in the Gospel

Pray and ask the Holy Spirit to enable you to truly know, believe, and trust that Jesus Christ’s life, death, and resurrection perfectly demonstrates how your needs are not too much for Him. In fact, He came because He saw your great need, which could not be solved with more knowledge or understanding.

Plug into Christ, who freely gives you all the knowledge, energy, and internal resources you need. He is the living water that does not run dry, so go to Him, drink, and be replenished.
