Type 9: The Peacmaker

9- The Peacemaker. It is called personality type Nine The Peacemaker because no type is more dedicated to the quest for internal and external peace for themselves and others. They are typically “spiritual seekers” who have a great want for connection with the cosmos, as well as with other people. They work to maintain their peace of mind just as they work to build peace and harmony in their world. The problems encountered in the Nine are fundamental to all psychological and spiritual work—being awake versus falling asleep to our actual nature; presence versus entrancement, openness versus blockage, tension versus relaxation, peace versuspain, union versus separation.

Type 9 in Found in the Bible

Isaac is very accommodating, conflict-avoidant, and even a little passive-aggressive. Even when he and his people put in a lot of work, the moment then people contend him for his space, he lets them have it.

This story repeats multiple times, with Isaac continuing to not advocate for himself or assert his presence, but still showing some of the attitude that simmers below the surface for nines when he names the places he left “Argument” and “Hostility.” He finally rests and honors God when space is made for him.

In other stories, we see Isaac with nine qualities when he is depicted to be more passive than other patriarchs, content to go along with other people’s desires, and prefers his older son Esau because his older son Esau provide him with food.

Type 9 Common Sins

Ones are conscientious and ethical, with a strong sense of right and wrong. They are teachers, crusaders, and advocates for change: always striving to improve things, but afraid of making a mistake. Well-organized, orderly, and fastidious, they try to maintain high standards, but can slip into being critical and perfectionistic. They typically have problems with resentment and impatience. At their Best: wise, discerning, realistic, and noble. Can be morally heroic.

Type 9 Freed in the Gospel

Pray and ask the Holy Spirit to enable you to truly know, believe, and trust that Jesus Christ’s life, death, and resurrection perfectly demonstrates that you matter to Him. This frees you from the need to merge with others to have an identity.

You bring radiance, joy, and real peace to everyone and everything. Your passions, opinions, and desires are all significant and can bless the world!
