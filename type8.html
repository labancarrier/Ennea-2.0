Type 8: The Challenger

8- The Challenger. It was named personality type Eight The Challenger because, of all the types, Eights enjoy challenging themselves as well as giving others opportunities that challenge them to exceed themselves in some way. Eights are charismatic and have the mental and bodily capacities to persuade others to follow them into all sorts of pursuits—from starting a company, to rebuilding a city, to running a household, to waging war, to making peace.

Type 8 Found in the Bible

Deborah was the only woman to serve as a judge over Israel in the book of Judges. She is shown to be strong, competent, and sure of herself. She exhibits eight qualities by being very comfortable with her own authority, as well as being very sure of the authority she sits under.

Healthy eights can both give and follow strong, clear leadership and direction. Deborah shows great confidence in God’s authority, thus making herself more effective and trustworthy for the people she was responsible for leading.

While other judges in the book did not handle power responsibly, Deborah demonstrates strength under control, as well as an ability to express a more vulnerable side in later stories.

Type 8 Common Sins

Lust- When used for good, Eights’ willpower and strength can be inspiring, uplifting, and empowering. However, sometimes it can be used as a form of protection against any emotional hurt that may come up. Their desire is to protect themselves and be in charge of their own life and if that is threatened to be taken away Eights can become abrasive and rebellious. They will do what they can to stay in charge and in control over their own lives.

Encouragement: You have immense strength and boldness and the Lord can use that to bring people to Jesus. It takes great strength to be vulnerable and it does mean you are weak, but it is through your weakness Christ’s power in you is displayed the most. “But he said to me, “My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness.” Therefore I will boast all the more gladly of my weaknesses, so that the power of Christ may rest upon me.” (2 Corinthians 12:9)

Type 8 Freed in the Gospel

Pray and ask the Holy Spirit to enable you to truly know, believe, and trust that Jesus Christ’s life, death, and resurrection perfectly demonstrates His love, power, and strength. He went to great lengths for you, and He won't allow anything to come between you now.

You cannot truly save yourself, but Christ can and did. This knowledge rescues you from being your own protector since Christ is your true armor. You can rest in His protection.
