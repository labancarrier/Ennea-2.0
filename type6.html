Type 6: The Loyalist

6- The Loyalist. It was named personality type Six The Loyalist because, of all the personality types, Sixes are the most loyal to their friends and to their ideals. They will “go down with the ship” and hang on to relationships of all sorts far longer than most other types. Sixes are also loyal to ideas, systems, and ideologies—even to the belief that all ideas or authorities should be questioned or defied. Indeed, not all Sixes go along with the “status quo”: their ideals may be rebellious and anti-authoritarian, even revolutionary. In any case, they will typically fight for their ideals more fiercely than they will fight for themselves, and they will defend their community or family more tenaciously than they will defend themselves.

Type 6 Found in the Bible

Esther’s story starts when she is in a dangerous situation, and she six depicted to be dutiful, concerned with safety and security, and loyal to her people, especially the authority figure, Mordecai, from her upbringing.

Esther is forward-thinking, very aware of the consequences if she were to rock the boat, and hesitant to do so.

However, she exemplifies the bravery of sixes by doing the very thing that is scary but necessary. She risks her own security for the sake of her people as a whole.

“If I perish, I perish” is the mantra of a six who has truly done their work to face their fear.

Type 6 Common Sins

Fear- When Sixes feel out of control with or begin to doubt themselves, they will try to control by playing out worst case scenarios. Sometimes this is out of preparation, but this can also be out of fear and trying to gain inner stability (the Sixes basic desire). If that stability that is ‘threatened’ in any way, whether it be uncertainty in a relationship or unable to come to a decision, they do all they can to create inner peace.

Encouragement: God is your safe place and your refuge. The Holy Spirit who raised Jesus from the dead lives inside you and the more you submit your thoughts to Him, the more you will be able to trust yourself. Feeling fear is normal, the question is what are you going to do with that fear? And will you let God’s perfect love cast it out? “But let all who take refuge in you rejoice; let them ever sing for joy, and spread your protection over them, that those who love your name may exult in you.” (Psalm 5:11)

Type 6 Freed in the Gospel

Pray and ask the Holy Spirit to enable you to truly know belief, and trust that Jesus Christ’s life, death, and resurrection perfectly demonstrate how all-powerful and sovereign He is. You can cast all your cares onto Him.

This also demonstrates that you are not alone or abandoned, for the God of the universe knows you, loves you, provides for you, protects you, and is with you always.
