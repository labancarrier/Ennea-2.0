Type 7: The Enthusiast

7- The Enthusiast. It was named this personality type The Enthusiast because Sevens are enthusiastic about almost anything that catches their attention. They approach life with curiosity, optimism, and a sense of adventure, like “kids in a candy store” who view the world in wide-eyed, fascinated anticipation of all the good things they are about to experience. They are bold and vivacious, striving for what they want in life with a cheerful determination. They have a quality best described by the Yiddish word “chutzpah”—a kind of brash “nerviness.”

Type 7 Found in the Bible

Solomon is known in the Bible as one of the wisest men in history. This story shows him receiving wisdom by taking his desire for more and channeling it towards a greater purpose. He exhibits seven qualities by being forward-thinking, looking several steps ahead, knowing just what he needs to maximize his situation.

Sevens have very sharp minds, and Solomon was no exception. We later see him as the builder/designer of the first temple, taking a beautiful vision and turning it into an extravagant reality.

In the end, Solomon’s legacy ends up being one of indulgence and excess, as he walks away from his great wisdom to be overcome by the riches of his kingdom and by the allure of women, similar to how sevens have a hard time embracing limits.

Type 7 Common Sins

Glutton- FOMO (fear of missing out) is experienced by many people, but is especially experienced on a deeper level for Sevens. They deeply desire to not miss out on the best life has to offer, and if that desire is not fulfilled in Christ then this can lead to them trying anything and everything to find the fulfillment they are looking for. This comes in ways of taking in every single experience they can get, while leaving the emotions inside unchecked and unacknowledged.

Encouragement: True contentment is not always found in finding what is out there in the world, but realizing you already have everything you need. Trust the Lord that He will always give you what you need and more. “You make known to me the path of life; in your presence there is fullness of joy; at your right hand are pleasures forevermore” (Psalm 16:11)

Type 7 Freed in the Gospel

Pray and ask the Holy Spirit to enable you to truly know, believe, and trust that Jesus Christ’s life, death, and resurrection perfectly demonstrates how He has taken care of all your needs, allowing you to experience true satisfaction and a contented heart. You are not alone, because He is with you.
